Our Story

Why Kew Gardens

Kew Gardens holds significance for us as it was the location for one of our early dates. We spent the cool autumn day wandering between the plants and the conservatories, ending at a nearby café in Richmond.

When we came back to tour the venue (which just happened to be on Valentine's Day), we accidentally wandered into the same café and decided that Kew would be the perfect location for our wedding.

It feels only fitting to begin this next chapter here, in a beautiful location surrounded by our family and closest friends.

The Venue

Cambridge Cottage, Kew Gardens

Cambridge Cottage is a historic former royal residence within Kew Gardens, surrounded by beautiful botanical landscapes. Originally built for the Duke of Cambridge, it now offers exclusive use as a uniquely special setting for celebrations. We encourage our guests to spend some time exploring the wider gardens, either before the ceremony or during cocktail hour. Guests will have access to the gardens for the entire day.

What to Wear

Dress Code & Weather

Garden Formal

Our ceremony and reception will take place in the gardens at Kew, so we suggest garden formal attire. If you'd like color inspiration, you may follow the color theme on the invitations or this wedding website.

Since we'll be outdoors for the afternoon, if you plan to wear heels we recommend block heels or wedges as the garden paths may be uneven.

Late August in London

Late August is one of London's warmest and sunniest times of year, and with the ceremony outdoors we're hoping to have lovely weather on the day. Average daytime temperatures are around 22–24°C (72–75°F for all the Americans in attendance).

That said, in England the weather can be unpredictable. We'd suggest bringing a light layer and an umbrella just in case.

P.S.

The Fox & The Deer

You may have noticed that we've included a fox and a deer on our invitation and wondered why. Both of our last names are derived from Yiddish, with Fooks meaning "fox" and Hersh (the root of Herscovici) meaning "deer".

Following our wedding we both plan to keep our last names, and remain, as ever, the couple of the fox and the deer.
